About This Dashboard

Last Updated: March 2026

Crash data is downloaded from the Texas Department of Transportation (TXDOT) website using the Crash Query Tool. All crash data in this dashboard represent those classified as alcohol-related and that occurred in the city of El Paso.

Definition of Alcohol-Related Crash

A crash is defined as alcohol-related if involved drivers or individuals were reported as “Under the Influence of Alcohol”, “Had Been Drinking”, or were reported to have a blood alcohol content (BAC) greater than 0.

Definition of Injury/Fatality

Injuries include people who were classified as having a suspected serious injury, suspected minor injury, or possible injury. Fatalities are defined as any motor vehicle traffic injury crash that results in the death of one or more persons within 30 days of the incident.

Definition of Intoxicated Driver

An intoxicated driver is a person who’s BAC test returned a value of 0.08g/dL or greater.

Acronyms

BAC = Blood alcohol content; YTD = Year to date

Technical Details
Calculation of Moving Average

Averages for crashes and fatal crashes between 2016 and Present were calculated using a 12-month moving average. This technique results in a smooth line, allowing the viewer to see if a trend is increasing or decreasing.

Holiday Analyses

Holiday crash statistics were calculated by aligning crash data to each holiday. For fixed-date holidays (e.g., New Year’s Day, Independence Day, Halloween, Christmas, and St. Patrick’s Day), each crash was assigned an event time based on the number of days before or after the calendar date of the holiday within each year. For shifting holidays (e.g., Super Bowl and Memorial Day), the holiday date was first computed for each year and then used as the reference point for event-time calculation.

In all cases, crashes were restricted to a ±7-day window around the holiday and then aggregated across years (2016–2025), excluding 2020 to avoid COVID-19-related disruptions. Average daily crash counts were then computed for each relative day to identify typical patterns before and after each holiday.

Missing Data

Missing data in the driver characteristics (such as gender and race/ethnicity) was minimal. Observations with missing or unknown values in these fields were excluded from analysis to ensure consistency and interpretability of demographic comparisons.
